Action Node Potentials

SA & AV Node Action Potential

Phase 4

  • Spontaneous depolarization due funny current (slow flow of Na)
  • Automaticity
  • Ach/Adenosine/Beta blockers/Digoxin/lvabradine decrease slope/HR
  • Catecholamines increase slope/HR

Phase 0

  • Depolarization
  • Opening of voltage gated Ca channel Inhibited by Non DHB CCB

Phase 3

  • Inactivation of Ca channels
  • Activation of K channels

Ventricular Action Potential

Phase 0: Depolarization

  • Na Channels open: Na goes into cell
  • Inhibited by class 1 Antiarrhythmics
    • Prolong QRS

Phase 1: Initial Repolarization

  • Na channels close
  • K channels begin to open

Phase 2: Plateau

  • Ca influx balances K efflux

Phase 3: Rapid Repolarization

  • Massive K efflux
  • Inhibited by class III Antiarrhythmics
  • Prolong QT interval

Phase 4

  • Resting potential

Anti-Arrhythmics Drugs

Class IA - Na Channel Blockers

Quinidine, Procainamide, Disopyramide

MOA: Block Na (and some K) channels

  • Prolong action potential, QRS (and sometimes QT interval)
  • Prolongs refractory period

Use: Reentry and ectopic SVT, V. Tach

SE:

  • Quinidine: Cinchonism (headache, tinnitus)
  • Procainamide: Drug induced SLE
  • Torsades de point due to QT prolongation

NOTE on all Class I antiarrhythmics

  • State dependent- meaning they affect conduction in frequently depolarized cells (tachycardia)

CLASS IA- Intermediate recovery time

Class IB - Na Channel Blockers

Lidocaine IV, Mexiletine PO

MOA: Block Na channels and decrease Action potential

  • Decrease QRS
  • Does not affect QT

Mainly affects Ischemic tissue (post MI)

Use: Ventricular arrhythmias post MI

SE:

  • CNS Depression
  • Cardiovascular collapse

CLASS IB- Fast recovery time

Class IC - Na Channel Blockers

Flecainide, Propafenone

MOA: Prolongs AP

Use: SVT, A. Fib Rhythm control

SE:

  • Contraindicated in structural heart disease and ischemic heart disease
  • Proarrhythmic

CLASS IC- Slow recovery time

Class II - Beta Blockers

  • БИСОГАММА Bisogamma 10 mg tablets
  • БИЗОХЕКСАЛ BisoHEXAL 5 mg tablets
  • КОНКОР Concor 5 mg tablets
  • ТИРЕЗ TYREZ 2.5 mg tablets
  • БЛОКБИС Blokbis 5/10 mg tablets

MOA: Decrease SA/AV Node by decreasing slope of phase 4

  • Particularly affect AV Node
  • Effect: Decrease HR and Prolongs PR interval

Antiarrhythmic Use

  • SVT
  • Rate control for A. fib

Cardiac Specific SE

  • AV Block

Class III - K Channel Blockers

Amiodarone, Ibutilide, Dofetilide, Sotalol

  • КОРДАРОН CORDARONE 200 mg tablets
  • КОРДАРОН КОРДАРОН амп. 3 мл.
  • АМИОДАРОН Amiodarone 200 mg tablets

MOA: Block K channels- prolong repolarization- Prolongs QT Interval

Use: Atrial fibrillation, A. Flutter, Ventricular tachycardia

SE: QT Prolongation and risk of torsades depoint

Class IV- Non DHB CCB

Verapamil, Diltiazem

Antiarrhythmic MOA: Slow conduction velocity in SA & AV node

MOA:

  • Nodal arrhythmias (SVT)
  • Rate control in A. fib

Cardiac specific SE:

  • Sinus node depression
  • AV Block
  • HF Exacerbation (Because of negative inotropic effect)

Other Antiarrhythmics

Adenosine

АДЕНОКОР Adenosine 3МГ/МЛ 2МЛ

MOA: Opens K channels and allows K out of cells- Hyperpolarizes the cell

  • Decreases AV conduction

Use: Terminating hemodynamically unstable SVT

Notes: Very short half life < 15 seconds

Side Effect

  • Transient asystole
  • Bronchospasm
  • Sense of impending doom

Wolff Parkinson White Syndrome

Abnormal fast accessory conduction pathway from atria to ventricles which bypasses the AV node and the ventricles depolarize earlier

ECG

  • Delta wave
  • Widened QRS
  • Short PR

Tx

  • Catheter ablation
  • Avoid AV Nodal blockers (Non DHB CCB, Digoxin, Metoprolol)
  • Can use Procainamide

Treatment of Arrhythmias

Atrial Fibrillation

  • Rate control (Preferred): Beta Blockers, Diltiazem, Amiodarone, Digoxin
  • Rhythm Control
  • Hemodynamically unstable: Cardioversion

Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT)

1st line: Vasovagal maneuver

2nd line: Beta Blockers, Diltiazem, Verapamil, Class I,III antiarrhythmics

3rd line: Cardioversion if hemodynamically unstable

  • Adenosine
  • Electrical cardioversion

Ventricular Tachycardia

Any of the antiarrhythmics: Usually Lidocaine or Amiodarone
