# Immunosuppressants
<!-- toc -->
## How do you Suppress the Immune System?
**Major Agents** of Immune Suppression
1. Glucocorticoids
2. Calcineurin inhibitors - Inhibit T-cells (IL-2)
3. Anti-metabolites - Stop DNA synthesis

**Minor Agents** of Immune Suppression
1. Modulation via Biologic Agents (TNF and IL-2 receptor Modulators)
2. Other

## Corticosteroids
*Glucocorticoids*

**Prednisone**
*MOA:* Inhibit NF-KB, Phospholipase A2, TNF, leukocyte adhesion

*Use:*
* Acute Organ Transplant
* Used for Chronic rejection; however, not beneficial long term!
* RA, MS, Acute Asthma/COPD, Gout, IBD, severe allergic reactions, etc.

*SE:*
* Cushing like syndrome
* Hyperglycemia
* Hypertension
* Osteoporosis
* Immunodeficiency (Infection)
* Ulcers
* Glaucoma
* Depression/Psychosis

**HPA Axis Suppression for long term > 10 days- Needs tapering if longer**

## Calcineurin Inhibitors
**Cyclosporin A**

*MOA:*
* Calcineurin Inhibitor
* Blocks T-cell proliferation by preventing IL-2 transcription

*Uses:* Prophylaxis of organ rejection and Treat graft-versus-host disease

*SE:*

* Nephrotoxicity, HTN HLD, neurotoxicity, gingival hyperplasia, hirsutism.
* Must monitor drug levels (small therapeutic window)
* Lots of Drug-drug and Drug food interactions

**Tacrolimus (FK506)**

*MOA:*
* Calcineurin inhibitor via binding FK506 binding protein (FKBP)
* Blocks T-cell proliferation by preventing IL-2 transcription

*Uses:* Prophylaxis of organ rejection and Treat graft-versus-host disease

*SE:*
* **Diabetes and neurotoxicity**
* No gingival hyperplasia or hirsutism

## Calcineurin-Like Inhibitors
**Sirolimus**
*MOA:*
* m-TOR inhibitor (Not Calcineurin inhibitors)
* Binds FKBP inhibits T-cell activation and B-cell differentiation by preventing response to IL-2

*Uses:*
* Prophylaxis of organ rejection and Treat graft-versus-host disease
* **Kidney transplant rejection prophylaxis**

*SE:* **Pancytopenia**, insulin resistance, hyperlipidemia, **not nephrotoxic**

**Tolerance - Dose increases needed over time**

*SIP-R agonist*
**Fingolimod (FTY720)**

*MOA:* Sequesters lymphocytes in the lymph nodes and Peyer's patches of the small intestines

*Uses:*
* Nowadays - Used for MS
* Historically - Adjunct in Transplant rejection

*SE:* Leukopenia and Bradycardia

## Anti-Metabolites
**Azathioprine**

*MOA:* Metabolized to 6MP (Purine analogue) -> Inhibits DNA synthesis

*Uses:* Rheumatoid arthritis, Crohn disease, glomerulonephritis, other Transplant rejection.

*SE:* Myelosuppression and hepatotoxicity

*DDI:* **Allopurinol (Inhibits Xanthine oxidase which metabolizes 6MP)
leads to higher levels of Azathioprine**

**Mycophenolate mofetil**
*MOA:* Reversibly inhibits IMP dehydrogenase, preventing purine synthesis of B and T cells.

*Uses:* Transplant rejection, Lupus

*SE:*
* Gl upset, **pancytopenia**, hypertension, hyperglycemia.
* Less nephrotoxic and neurotoxic.

## Anti-Tumor Necrosis Factor Alpha (TNF-a)

*Anti-TNFa*
**Infliximab & Certolizumab & Adalimumab**
*MOA:* Binds and neutralizes TNF

*Use:* IBD, RA, ankylosing spondylitis, psoriasis.

*SE:*
* Related to TNF-a
  * Immunosuppression
  * **Reactivation of TB (no TNF from macrophages to wall off the bacteria) - Need baseline TB test**
* Related to the drug being an antibody
  * Antibodies to the drug
  * Infusion reaction

**Etanercept**
* Fusion Inhibitor (Not MAB)
* Fuses with TNF receptor and inactivates it - Does NOT bind to TNF.

## Monoclonal Antibody Nomenclature

**Is it Human?**

* Mouse: -o-
* Chimeric: -xi- (80%)
* Humanized: -zu- (90%)
* (Fully) Human: -u- (100%)

**Systems**
* **Cardiovascular** -> -cir- or -ci-

Example: Bevacizumab & Abciximab
* **Immune System** -> -li- or -I-

Example: Infliximab & adalimumab
* **Cancer** -> -tu-

Example: Trastuzumab & Rituximab

## Interleukin 2 (IL2) Agonist and Antagonist
### IL2 Receptor Antagonist
**Daclizumab and Basiliximab**

*MOA:* Block interleukin-2 receptor on T-lymphocytes

*Use:*

* **Adjunct for prevention of acute transplant rejection**
  * Used with steroids and cyclosporine
* MS

*SE:* Liver injury & **Infections**

### IL-2 Receptor Agonist
**Aldesleukin**

*MOA:*
* Promotes T-cell proliferation
* **Increases body's ability to detect and respond to cancers**

*Use:* Malignant melanoma and renal cell cancer

*SE:* **Hypotension**

## Miscellaneous Monoclonals
### Muromonab-CD3 (Orthoclone OKT3)
*MOA:* **Binds CD3 glycoprotein on T lymphocytes** and located next TCR and blocks this site too

*Use:* Prophylaxis and Acute organ rejections

*SE:* Cytokine release syndrome (fever, chills, vomiting, headache, weakness)

### Rh (d) immune globulin
*MOA:* Block antibody response of an Rh-negative mother to her Rh-positive baby.

*Use:* **Hemolytic disease of the newborn (erythroblastosis fetalis)**
* Suppresses the mother's immune response from attacking Rh-positive blood cells of the
baby which have entered her bloodstream. Prevents hemolytic disease in Rh-positive newborns.

## Growth Factors
### Erythropoietin Analogs (**RBCs**)
**Epoetin Alfa**

*MOA:*
* Stimulates red blood cell production
* Erythropoietin Analog

*Use:* **Anemia, Renal failure, Cancer chemotherapy, and chronic inflammatory conditions**

*SE:* **Hypertension & Myeloproliferative Disorder**

### Granulocyte Stimulating factors (**WBCs**)
**Filgrastim & Sargramostim**

*MOA:*
* Filgrastim **(Granulocyte colony stimulating factor - G-CSF)**
* Sargramostim **(Granulocyte-macrophage colony stimulating factor - GM-CSF)**

Use: Chemotherapy-induced neutropenia

*SE:* **Bone pain** in lower back, sternum and pelvis.

## Other Randoms (Low-Yield)
### Bacillus Calmette-Guerin (BCG)
* Attenuated strain of Mycobacterium bovis
* TB Vaccine given in Europe
### Thymic Factors

*MOA:* Promotes T lymphocyte and early stem cells differentiation

*Use:* Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)

### Interferons
*MOA:* Interferes with viral protein synthesis

*Use:* Hairy cell leukemia, Kaposi's sarcoma, malignant melanomas

*SE:* ***Depression***, **Neutropenia**, Myopathy

*Side note*
* Interferon Alpha- Tx viral and cancer stuff
* Interferon Beta- Tx Multiple Sclerosis
* Interferon Gamma- Chronic Granulomatous Disease
